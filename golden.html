About Golden Temple

The Golden Temple, also known as Sri Harmandir Sahib, is the spiritual center of Sikhism located in Amritsar, Punjab. Built in 1604 by Guru Arjan Dev Ji, it stands as a symbol of equality, peace, and brotherhood. Its golden façade shimmering over the sacred Amrit Sarovar creates a mesmerizing reflection that draws millions of devotees and tourists every year.

Top Attractions Around Amritsar

Jallianwala Bagh

A historical memorial that stands as a reminder of India’s struggle for independence.

Wagah Border

Witness the electrifying flag-lowering ceremony between India and Pakistan at this iconic border post.

Akal Takht

The seat of Sikh temporal authority, symbolizing courage and justice beside the Golden Temple.

Durgiana Temple

A beautiful Hindu temple with architecture resembling the Golden Temple, dedicated to Goddess Durga.
