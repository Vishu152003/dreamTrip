About Jim Corbett National Park

Located in Uttarakhand, Jim Corbett National Park is India’s oldest national park and a haven for wildlife lovers. Established in 1936, it is renowned for its Bengal tigers, elephants, and diverse flora and fauna. Spread across 500 sq km, Corbett offers thrilling jungle safaris, birdwatching, and serene stays in nature lodges — making it one of India’s best eco-tourism destinations.

Top Attractions to Visit

Dhikala Zone

The most popular safari zone, home to tigers, elephants, and breathtaking landscapes.

Garjiya Devi Temple

A sacred temple located on a large rock in the Kosi River — a must-visit for peace seekers.

Corbett Waterfall

A serene waterfall surrounded by dense teak forest, perfect for picnics and relaxation.

Bird Watching

Spot over 600 bird species including kingfishers, crested eagles, and Himalayan bulbuls.
